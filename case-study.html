The Challenge

Selling a $999 bag to a stranger on social media

A near-$1,000 product demands trust before it demands a purchase. Cold traffic on Meta won't simply buy — they need to be shown, convinced, and retargeted with precision before opening their wallets.

Execuluxe had the product, the reviews, and the brand story. What they lacked was a structured paid media system — no consistent ad spend, no funnel architecture, no video creative built to stop the scroll and drive clicks on a luxury price point.

The brief: build it from scratch, make it profitable, and prove it at scale.

The Echobroad Strategy

Product-led video + layered audience targeting

  • Hero video creative leading with the bag's signature transformation — tote to rollerbrief — designed to hook within the first 2 seconds of a Reel
  • Pain-point copywriting: targeted the frustration of heavy bags, chaotic travel, and being forced to sacrifice elegance for function
  • Cold interest stacks combining executive women, business travel, luxury fashion, and female entrepreneurs across the USA
  • Warm retargeting re-engaged video viewers (50%+ watch) and product page visitors with social proof and urgency
  • Q4 gifting season timing maximised purchase intent — self-gifting professionals and partners buying high-value gifts
3-Phase Funnel Architecture
01
Awareness — Stop the Scroll
Short-form Reels and Stories targeting broad interest audiences of professional US women. Creative led with the bag transformation. KPI: 3-second view rate, video completion, CPM efficiency.
02
Consideration — Build Desire
Retargeted warm audiences — video viewers and website visitors — with customer reviews, unboxing content, and lifestyle creatives. Pushed traffic to the product page with feature-focused messaging.
03
Conversion — Close the Sale
Tight retargeting on cart abandoners and product page visitors. Urgency-led messaging around holiday shipping deadlines and bundle offers. Direct-to-checkout CTAs. Lowest CPA, highest intent audience.

The Challenge

You can't smell an ad. So we made people feel it.

Fragrance is the most sensory-dependent product category in retail. You cannot demo a scent on Meta. Conversion lives entirely in emotional storytelling and the power of your creative to create desire without the primary sense.

Elysian had built genuine product love organically — an award-winning product, glowing reviews, and a deeply personal brand story. But their reach was limited to whoever stumbled upon them.

The mission: scale new customer acquisition on Meta while preserving the brand's intimate, artisan soul — and do it at a cost that made the numbers work.

The Echobroad Strategy

Sensory storytelling + award credibility + volume efficiency

  • "Best New Fragrance 2024" — leading every cold-traffic ad with the award hook gave an indie brand instant third-party credibility in the first 2 seconds
  • Sensory visual language: close-up bottle shots, liquid pour, atmospheric candlelight — if you can't smell it, make every frame make you want to
  • UGC-style creative leveraged real customer voices and authentic reviews to build trust with audiences unfamiliar with the brand
  • Interest targeting into niche fragrance communities, luxury beauty, indie perfume lovers, and gifting audiences during Q4
  • Retargeting upsell loop — visitors who sampled discovery sets were re-engaged with full-bottle creatives to compound LTV
3-Phase Funnel Architecture
01
Hook — Lead with the Award
Every cold-traffic ad opened with "Best New Fragrance 2024" — an instant trust signal for a brand the audience had never heard of. Targeting: niche fragrance enthusiasts, luxury beauty, indie perfume communities, USA, 22–48.
02
Volume — Maximise Purchases
At $169 per bottle, the price was premium but accessible. Creatives showed product beauty, customer reviews, and the brand story. The $46.50 CPA was achieved by targeting high-intent fragrance buyers who needed little convincing once shown the right creative.
03
Scale — Compound LTV
Past buyers and warm audiences were retargeted with new scent launches and collection ads. Every new purchase added to the pool. The 449 purchases weren't just conversions — they were the foundation of a long-term repeat customer base.
